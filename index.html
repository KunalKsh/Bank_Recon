🔄 3-Phase Matching Logic

PHASE 1: Primary Payment Match

Try to match bank transactions against the actual payment received date and amount.

For each NAV record: - Take Payment Date (Column 5) + PAYMENT Amount (Column 6) - Search through bank transactions - Apply P0-P4 matching rules - STOP at first match (one-to-one mapping)
Goal: Match the money that was actually received to the policy payment

PHASE 2: Expected Due Dates

If Phase 1 doesn't match, check if payment arrived against one of the 6 expected due dates.

For each unmatched NAV record: - Check all 6 due date pairs: • 1st Due Date (Col 11) + 1st Due Amount (Col 12) • 2nd Due Date (Col 13) + 2nd Due Amount (Col 14) • ... through 6th Due (Col 21-22) - Apply P0-P4 logic to each - STOP at first match
Goal: Find if payment arrived at expected due dates instead

PHASE 3: Broad Recovery

Last resort: use wide tolerance on the original payment date to catch late arrivals.

For each remaining unmatched NAV: - Use Payment Date (Col 5) + PAYMENT (Col 6) - Apply BROAD matching rules: • P5: -2 to +4 days window, ±25 variance • P6: -2 to +7 days window, ±100 variance
Goal: Recovery of delayed/partial payments

Decision Flow for Each NAV Record

1️⃣ Phase 1: Match Payment Date? → YES ✓ MATCHED (PHASE 1)
↓ NO
2️⃣ Phase 2: Match any Due Date? → YES ✓ MATCHED (PHASE 2)
↓ NO
3️⃣ Phase 3: Broad recovery? → YES ✓ MATCHED (PHASE 3)
↓ NO
❌ UNMATCHED - Manual Review Required

⚙️ P0-P4 Core Matching Rules

These rules are applied in Phases 1, 2, and optionally Phase 3:

P0: Direct Alignment (Highest Confidence)

Bank Date = Expected Date AND Bank Amount = Expected Amount

Example: Bank on Mar-1, ₱18,298.75 = Payment Date Mar-1, Amount ₱18,298.75

P1: Decimal Offset

Bank Date = Expected Date AND |Bank Amount - Expected Amount| = ₱1.00

Example: Bank ₱23,000.00 vs Expected ₱22,999.00 (off by ₱1)

P2: Fee Deduction

Bank Date = Expected Date AND Amount difference = ₱5, ₱10, ₱15, or ₱20

Example: Bank ₱9,980 vs Expected ₱10,000 (fee ₱20)

P3: Bank Latency

Bank Date = Expected Date + 1 day AND Bank Amount = Expected Amount (exact)

Example: Bank on Mar-2, Amount exact = Payment expected Mar-1

P4: Temporal/Rounding

Bank Date between (Expected Date - 2 days) and (Expected Date + 5 days) AND ROUND(Bank Amount) = ROUND(Expected Amount)

Example: Bank Mar-3, ₱10,000.49 vs Expected Feb-28, ₱10,000.51 (both round to ₱10,000)

P5 & P6 (Phase 3 Only)

P5 (Tight): -2 to +4 days window, ±₱25 variance
P6 (Broad): -2 to +7 days window, ±₱100 variance

🔍 UID Duplicate Tracking

What is a UID?

UID = Unique Identifier = Bank Date + Bank Amount combination

UID Format: "YYYY-MM-DD|₱Amount" Example: "2026-04-30|₱23000.00"

Why Track Duplicates?

Scenario 1: One bank transaction (₱50,000 on Apr-30) matches TWO different policies → Batch payment
Scenario 2: Duplicate entry in bank statement → Same UID appears twice
Scenario 3: Split payment across phases → One bank entry matches Phase 1 AND Phase 2

How It Works

1. For each matched pair (Bank ↔ NAV), capture the Bank UID
2. Count how many different NAV records matched the same UID
3. If count > 1: FLAG as duplicate ⚠️
4. Show: "Bank UID matched 3 policies" for manual review

Example Duplicate Scenario

BANK STATEMENT: 2026-04-30 | ₱50,000.00 ← Single bank entry (UID: 2026-04-30|50000) NAV LEDGER Matches: Policy IBC-9271 | Payment Date 2026-04-30 | ₱25,000 → MATCH (Phase 1) Policy IXD-1346 | 1st Due Date 2026-04-30 | ₱25,000 → MATCH (Phase 2) Policy IRN-3403 | 2nd Due Date 2026-05-01 | ₱24,980 → MATCH (Phase 4) RESULT: ⚠️ UID "2026-04-30|50000" matched 3 records ACTION: Manual review required - is this a batch payment?
